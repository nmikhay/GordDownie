Gordon Edgar Downie

Early Life

Gordon Edgar Downie was born in Amherstview, Ontario, and raised in Kingston, Ontario, along with his brothers Mike and Patrick, and sisters Charlyn and Paula. He was the son of Lorna (Neal) and Edgar Charles Downie, a travelling salesman, later a real estate broker and developer. In Kingston, Downie attended the downtown high school Kingston Collegiate and Vocational Institute, where other members of the Tragically Hip also attended. In high school, Downie was the frontman for a band called the Slinks performing at the KCVI Variety show and rivaling older members Rob Baker and Gord Sinclair's band the Rodents. After graduating from high school, Downie attended Queen's University where he majored in film studies, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts and Science in 1986.

Career

In 1984, at age 20, Downie formed the Tragically Hip with Rodents's members Rob Baker and Gord Sinclair, another younger Kingston Collegiate and Vocational Institute alumnus, Johnny Fay, and saxophonist Davis Manning.[6] In 1986, Manning left the band as guitarist-vocalist Paul Langlois joined. Originally, the band covered popular British rock songs from the 1960s. In an interview with Canadian music journalist Steve Newton, Downie noted that the Tragically Hip's early setlist was originally drawn to bands such as The Yardbirds and The Stones, a decision that was made because the Hip wished other Kingston bar bands would also play the genre.The Tragically Hip quickly became famous once MCA Records president Bruce Dickinson saw them performing at the Horseshoe Tavern in Toronto and offered them a record deal.

Downie began pursuing a solo career with the release of Coke Machine Glow in 2001. He published his first poetry and prose collection alongside the album and under the same title. The backing musicians, credited as the Goddamned Band, consisted of indie rock band the Dinner Is Ruined, Josh Finlayson of Skydiggers and singer-songwriter Julie Doiron. He released his second solo album, Battle of the Nudes, in 2003 before returning to the studio with the Tragically Hip. His third solo effort, The Grand Bounce, was released in 2010. Both it and Battle of the Nudes are credited as Gord Downie and the Country of Miracles.

Fun Facts

Some Interesting Facts

Trailer Park Boys

He appeared in two episodes of Trailer Park Boys as well as in their self-titled film. The show’s characters appeared in the Hip video for “The Darkest One,” with Downie negotiating a deal: a stolen car in exchange for “two buckets of chicken and a drive to the liquor store".

Renewable Energy Supporter

Back in 2005, Downie became an early customer of Bullfrog Power, a renewable energy company. According to a 2015 Canadian Manufacturing article, Bullfrog has provided power to The Tragically Hip since 2007.

Bithday

Downie’s birthday of Feb. 6 is shared by the legendary Bob Marley, Guns N’ Roses (and now AC/DC) singer Axl Rose and the immortal Rick Astley.

Balloon Stomping Humor

During a performance of “At The Hundredth Meridian” at The Tragically Hip’s millennium eve show at Toronto’s Air Canada Centre, Downie proceeded to stomp on hundreds of balloons that had descended after midnight. “This is genocide,” Downie quipped during the stomping.

Eagle Feather Honor

Downie was honored with an eagle feather at the Assembly of First Nations, Quebec for his contribution and support of the Indigenous people of Canada.

The Last Show

In the summer months of 2016, The Tragically Hip toured around Canada on the release of their 14th studio album, 'Man Machine Poem'. When the last concert was streamed live on radio, television, and the internet, more than 11.7 million people experienced the music from the comfort of their homes.

Death and Reactions

Gord Downie, famed musician and member of the Tragically Hip, passed away from glioblastoma, a type of brain cancer, at the age of 53 in Toronto on October 17, 2017. The next morning, the Tragically Hip shared a heartfelt statement from his family on their website, highlighting Gord's passion for music, his gratitude for his life, and the effort he dedicated to being a good family man.

Canada mourned his loss deeply. Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, alongside numerous public figures and fans, paid tribute to Downie, acknowledging the significant impact his music had on the nation. The news of his death led to a nationwide outpouring of grief, from Twitter and Facebook to physical gatherings and silent tributes.

Residents of Bobcaygeon, an Ontario village Downie put on the map with his music, held a candlelight vigil in his honor. In Kingston, Downie's hometown, a large public gathering was held, and Mayor Bryan Paterson expressed the city's sorrow through public gestures of mourning.

Canadian radio stations responded heavily, with many increasing the band's airplay significantly, some temporarily rebranding as "Gord FM" in Downie's honor. CTV rescheduled the premiere of "Long Time Running", a documentary about the Tragically Hip's 2016 tour, to pay tribute to Downie, and CBC aired Downie's solo concert "Secret Path".

Dr. Arjun Sahgal, a prominent oncologist, lauded Downie's courage, equating his contribution to cancer awareness and indigenous reconciliation issues to the impact of modern-day Terry Fox. Gord Downie's passing left an indelible mark on Canada, affirming his legacy as a significant cultural figure.
